Precision Location Engine
// resolving a farmer's exact plot — county is just the start
Why this matters Murang'a County alone spans altitudes from 900m to over 3,000m and crosses multiple agro-ecological zones — coastal lowlands, mid-altitude slopes, and high-altitude tea zones all within the same county boundaries. Recommending "plant tea" to everyone in Murang'a is not just inaccurate — it can cause real financial harm to farmers in unsuitable zones. This precision engine resolves each query to a 30m × 30m micro-cell before any crop model runs.
LAYER 1
County
Administrative County
Farmer selects or confirms one of Kenya's 47 counties via WhatsApp/USSD menu. Used only as a starting boundary — never as the final resolution for crop advice.
~13,000 km²
LAYER 2
Sub-County
Sub-County Selection
System presents a short list of sub-counties within the selected county. Farmer picks theirs — or types a well-known town/centre nearby for automatic matching.
~800–2,500 km²
LAYER 3
Ward
Electoral Ward Resolution
Kenya has 1,450 wards with defined boundaries. The system resolves the farmer to a specific ward using their typed nearest centre or GPS. This level drives most soil/AEZ pre-filtering.
~50–300 km²
LAYER 4
GPS Point
Farmer's Exact GPS Coordinates
Farmers with smartphones can share their live location via WhatsApp (or a USSD callback that reads device GPS). Feature phones fall back to ward-centroid with a ±500m tolerance flag on the advisory.
±10m accuracy
LAYER 5
Micro-AEZ Cell
Agro-Ecological Zone Grid Lookup
GPS coordinates are snapped to Kenya's FAO GAEZ micro-AEZ raster (1km resolution) and iSDAsoil's 30m soil grid. Altitude, slope, and aspect are extracted from a 30m DEM (SRTM). This determines which sub-AEZ the plot belongs to — even within a single ward, plots on a north-facing slope vs a valley bottom get different recommendations.
1 km → 30m grid
LAYER 6
Plot Profile
Full Plot Intelligence Package
Final resolved data bundle: altitude band, slope %, aspect (N/S/E/W), AEZ sub-zone, soil pH + NPK + texture + depth, 3-year NDVI trend, drainage class, and mean annual rainfall for that exact cell. This bundle is what feeds the crop suitability model.
The ML Input
🏔️ Altitude Banding
Crops are modelled by altitude band (e.g. 0–900m, 900–1500m, 1500–2100m, 2100m+). Even a 200m elevation difference within a ward can shift from maize country to Irish potato territory.
🧭 Aspect & Slope Analysis
A north-facing slope in Kenya receives less direct sunlight and retains moisture longer — favouring different crops than a south-facing slope at the same altitude. Extracted from SRTM 30m DEM via GDAL.
🌿 NDVI History
3-year Sentinel-2 NDVI time-series for the 30m cell reveals historical productivity, seasonal vegetation stress, and water availability patterns — helping identify consistently high or low-yield plots.
💧 Drainage Class
Waterlogging risk is inferred from soil texture, slope, and historical rainfall. A flat clay-heavy plot at the base of a hill gets flood-tolerant crop recommendations and drainage management guidance.
📡 Nearest Weather Station
Farmer's GPS is matched to the nearest active KMD station using Haversine distance. Removes county-averaging bias — a farmer in Maragua gets Maragua station data, not a Murang'a county average.
🗺️ Ward-Level Crop History
Historical yield data aggregated per ward from Ministry of Agriculture reports is used to calibrate the expected yield range for each crop — ground-truthing satellite and model estimates against real outcomes.

Greenhouse Intelligence Pathway
// a separate, parallel advisory track for controlled-environment farmers
Design rationale Greenhouse farmers operate in partially or fully controlled environments. Ambient weather — rainfall, wind, outdoor temperature — is significantly decoupled from their growing conditions. Routing them through the standard field-farmer pipeline would generate misleading advice: recommending drought-tolerant crops because outdoor rainfall is low, or warning about pest outbreaks driven by wind patterns that can't penetrate a polyhouse. This pathway replaces weather as the primary input with internal environment parameters and focuses advisory energy on the variables that actually determine greenhouse outcomes.
🏠 What Changes for Greenhouse Farmers
The greenhouse pathway uses the same location resolution engine (GPS → ward → micro-AEZ) to establish soil context and nearest market — but weather data shifts from primary driver to a secondary risk signal (extreme heat waves, frost risk, hailstorm risk that could damage structure). The core model stack is rebuilt around controlled-environment variables: internal temperature and humidity management, irrigation scheduling, growing medium, crop cycles per year, and disease vectors that thrive in enclosed humid environments.
🌡️
Module 01 — Environment
Internal Climate Intelligence
Replaces outdoor weather as the primary input. Focuses on internal temperature and humidity management — the biggest levers for greenhouse yield and disease.
Optimal internal temperature bands per crop (day/night differential)
Humidity management schedule — ventilation timing, shade net recommendations
External weather used only for structural risk (hail, frost warning if temp drops below 5°C)
Integrated with optional IoT sensor inputs (temp, humidity, CO₂ sensors)
💧
Module 02 — Irrigation
Precision Irrigation Scheduler
Outdoor rainfall is irrelevant. Irrigation scheduling is driven entirely by crop water demand, growing medium, and growth stage — not the sky.
Drip irrigation schedule per crop × growth stage (germination, vegetative, flowering, fruiting)
Growing medium consideration: soil, hydroponics, coco coir, peat — different water retention
ET₀ (evapotranspiration) estimation from internal temp + crop type, not rainfall
Water quality advisory — EC and pH target ranges for irrigation water per crop
🧪
Module 03 — Nutrition
Fertigation & Nutrition Planner
Greenhouse crops can be intensively managed. The system provides a full fertigation schedule — what nutrients, in what ratios, at each growth stage.
Macro and micro-nutrient schedule (N-P-K-Ca-Mg-S + trace elements) per crop type
Adjusted for growing medium — hydroponic systems need full nutrient solutions
Nutrient deficiency identification via symptom description (visual checklist in chatbot)
Locally available fertilizer recommendations (KEPHIS-registered products only)
🦟
Module 04 — Pest & Disease
Enclosed Environment Pathogen Advisor
Greenhouse pest/disease profiles differ fundamentally from field farming. Whitefly, spider mite, botrytis, and powdery mildew thrive in enclosed humid environments — not Desert Locust.
Separate pest/disease library for greenhouse crops (tomato, capsicum, cucumber, French beans)
Risk scoring based on internal humidity × temperature — peak botrytis risk flagged
Biological control recommendations (predatory mites, beneficial insects) alongside chemicals
PCPB-approved chemicals that are safe for enclosed application (phytotoxicity risk flags)
📦
Module 05 — Crop Selection & ROI
Greenhouse Crop Profitability Ranker
Greenhouse crop choice is driven by yield per square metre, cycles per year, and market price — not by outdoor AEZ. The model optimises for indoor intensity.
Crop list tailored to greenhouse production: tomatoes, capsicum, cucumber, French beans, herbs, strawberries, baby corn
Yield per m² × cycles per year × harvest price = annual revenue model
Greenhouse size input (small: <200m², medium: 200–500m², large: 500m²+) for scaled ROI
Market distance and export potential considered (French beans for export vs. local tomatoes)
📅
Module 06 — Production Calendar
Year-Round Planting Scheduler
Unlike field farmers who are bound by rain seasons, greenhouse farmers can plant year-round. The system builds an optimised annual production calendar for maximum revenue.
Cycle planning: staggered planting schedules to ensure continuous harvest and cash flow
Price seasonality awareness — schedule harvests to hit market price peaks (e.g. tomato peaks in dry season)
Crop rotation within greenhouse to prevent soil-borne disease build-up
Task calendar: weekly and monthly reminders for pruning, spraying, harvesting, fertigation

ML Models — Deep Dive
// architecture, training details, and greenhouse additions
Model 01 — Suitability
Hyper-Local Crop Classifier
Algorithm: XGBoost Multiclass + Random Forest Ensemble for 30+ Kenyan crops, trained with ward-level stratification
Training data: KARI agronomic trials (1980–2024), Ministry of Agriculture ward yield reports, FAO GAEZ Kenya, farmer survey data
Output: Top-5 ranked crops with confidence %, yield estimate per acre (altitude-adjusted), water need, and micro-AEZ suitability score
Update cycle: Retrained each season with new farmer outcome data, geo-stratified cross-validation to prevent county-level overfitting
Input Features (Hyper-Local)
Altitude Band (m) Slope % Aspect Drainage Class AEZ Sub-Zone Soil pH NPK Ratio Soil Texture 3yr NDVI Mean Ward Yield History Rainfall (nearest station) Temp Min/Max Season
Model 02 — Pricing
Market-Distance Adjusted Forecaster
Algorithm: Facebook Prophet for seasonality + LSTM for non-linear trend capture. Market-distance weighting applied post-model.
Training data: KNBS 10-year county market prices, AMIS East Africa commodity data, transport cost matrix per county
Output: Farm-gate price estimate = market price − transport cost(distance to farmer's nearest market). Confidence bands at 30/60/90/120-day horizons.
Update cycle: Weekly re-fit with latest market prices; transport cost matrix updated monthly
Input Features
Historical Prices Planting Season Nearest Market (km) Rainfall Forecast County Supply Import/Export Data Inflation CPI
Model 03 — Pest Risk
Geo-Filtered Pest Probability Scorer
Algorithm: Logistic Regression + Random Forest per pest species; separate model library for field vs greenhouse environments
Training data: CABI historical outbreak records, KEPHIS surveillance data, EMPRES-i geo-tagged alerts
Output: Risk score 0–100 per pest per crop; field model uses outdoor climate + nearby outbreaks within 80km; greenhouse model uses internal humidity + temperature
Field Pest Features
Temperature Humidity % Wind Direction AEZ Zone Nearby Outbreaks (80km) Rainfall Anomaly
Greenhouse Pest Features
Internal Humidity Internal Temp Ventilation Frequency Crop Density Days Since Last Spray
Model 04 — Advisory
Dual-Mode LLM Recommendation Engine
Approach: RAG pipeline feeding ML outputs into Claude as structured context + Kenya-specific knowledge base. Two system prompt modes: Field and Greenhouse.
Knowledge Base: PCPB pesticide registry, KARI crop guides, Ministry of Agriculture extension notes, greenhouse production manuals for Kenya
Field output: Crop choice citing specific altitude/soil/AEZ context + planting calendar + pesticide plan + expected income
Greenhouse output: Best crop for greenhouse size + annual production calendar + fertigation schedule + pest/disease plan + revenue projection
Field System Prompt Context
Plot Profile Altitude Band Slope & Aspect Crop Scores Adj. Price Forecast Pest Risk Farmer Budget
Greenhouse System Prompt Context
GH Size & Type Irrigation System Current Crop Pest Risk (Internal) Market Distance Growing Medium
